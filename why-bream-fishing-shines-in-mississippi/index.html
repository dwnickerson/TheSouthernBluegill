Why Bream Fishing Shines in Mississippi

D.W. Published on December 2, 2025

Mississippi's diverse freshwater systems—from the muddy Mississippi River oxbows to the clear state-managed lakes—create ideal habitats for bream. These sunfish thrive in shallow, vegetated areas rich in insects, snails, and forage. Bluegill, with their signature ear flap, are the acrobatic acrobats of the group, while redear sunfish (aka shellcrackers) add muscle with their powerful pulls. In our state, you can expect catches averaging 6-8 inches, but don't be surprised by 1-pound bluegills or 2-pound redears that make for epic tales.

The best part? Bream are forgiving for all skill levels. Kids love the constant action, and adults appreciate the relaxation. Plus, they're plentiful—our lakes are stocked and managed by the Mississippi Department of Wildlife, Fisheries, and Parks (MDWFP) to ensure healthy populations year after year.
