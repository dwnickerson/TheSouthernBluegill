Plain-English parameter guide

Turbidity (NTU) and Visibility: 0 NTU means very clear water. Around 150-300+ NTU means muddy water with very low light penetration. Visibility is how far you can see into the pond (roughly Secchi-style). Higher visibility means clearer water; lower means murkier water.

Model Snapshot Time: FishCast now locks calculations to the same local hour (default 12:00 noon) so your model estimate compares to observations taken at that same time of day.

Wind Reduction Factor: 1.0 means full grid wind reaches the pond; 0.0 means all wind is blocked. Lower values reduce wind-driven cooling and evaporation.

Evaporation Coefficient: Scales latent heat loss from wind + dry air. Higher values make the pond cool faster in breezy dry weather.

Albedo / Reflectivity: Fraction of sunlight reflected. Higher albedo means less solar heat absorbed by water.

Longwave Factor: Tunes infrared heat exchange with sky/clouds; higher values increase nighttime IR cooling sensitivity.

Stratification Layers: 1 = single mixed pond; 2-3 layers allow surface and deep water to diverge with diffusion between layers.

Inflow Temperature: Temperature of incoming water (groundwater, runoff, spring feed). This improves flow-driven heating/cooling realism.

Shading and Fetch: More shade reduces solar gain. Longer fetch increases wave action and wind cooling efficiency.

Sediment Conductivity + Sediment Depth: Adds a more physical bottom heat-storage term beyond the simple sediment factor.

Daily Alpha vs Mix Alpha: Daily alpha controls how quickly bulk pond temp approaches daily equilibrium; mix alpha controls vertical layer blending speed.

Auto-calibration + Uncertainty: Auto-calibration can tune selected parameters to reduce MAE on saved observations. Uncertainty band shows likely range around each estimate.
